About me

I am a first year PhD student focusing on Empirical Software Research with Prof. Prem Devanbu. My past, present and quite likely future work focuses on creating expressive and predictive models of real-world software (see also my publications), which fits squarely in my research interests of studying software properties to improve the ease of its development. I believe that the next milestone of computational intelligence should be empowering software to collaborate on software itself.

I enjoy travelling and collaborating with other groups, and will always reply to an idea, no matter how odd (no really). On the side, I try to do my share by (sub-)reviewing papers, and keep myself entertained by discussing/implementing ideas ranging from the absurd to the bizarre (yet sometimes intriguing; corresponding blog may come).
